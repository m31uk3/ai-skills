## Background

Dexter Horthy is the founder and CEO of HumanLayer, a Y Combinator-backed startup (Fall 2024 batch) focused on human-in-the-loop solutions for AI agents. He started coding at age 17 at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) and spent seven years at Replicated, a Series C developer tools startup, where he held roles spanning Engineer, SE, PM, and Executive. At Replicated, he worked on container orchestrators and helped teams like HashiCorp, DataStax, and H2O.ai ship on-prem Kubernetes products.

## Key Contributions

- **HumanLayer / CodeLayer**: Founded HumanLayer to provide human-in-the-loop approval workflows for AI agents. The company's product, CodeLayer, is an open-source IDE for managing coding agents, built with proven workflows to solve hard problems in complex codebases.
- **12-Factor Agents**: Authored the influential "12-Factor Agents" framework — a set of patterns for building reliable LLM applications, distilled from conversations with roughly 100 founders, AI engineers, and CTOs building agents deployed in production.
- **Origin story**: Initially aimed to build an AI-powered SQL database management system. Realized the risks of unsupervised AI control led to the creation of HumanLayer — a safety layer that requires human approval before AI agents take consequential actions.

## Philosophy & Thought Leadership

Dexter is known for his views on scaling coding agents, including why back-and-forth prompting fails, how spec-first development keeps teams aligned, and why "everything is context engineering." He advocates for deploying AI agents into existing workflows (Slack, email) rather than forcing users into new interfaces.
