## Background

Zach Lloyd is the founder and CEO of Warp, an intelligent terminal reimagined as an agentic development environment. Before founding Warp (approximately five years ago), Zach was a principal engineer at Google, where he worked on Google Sheets and Google Docs. He was also the co-founder and CTO of Selfmade. He holds a degree from Stanford University.

## Key Contributions

- **Warp terminal**: Built a modern reimagination of the terminal that has evolved into "a terminal with agents built in." Warp ranked in the top five on SWE-bench and #1 on Terminal Bench.
- **Agentic development environment**: Transformed Warp from a terminal tool into a full agent platform where natural language triggers AI agents that can write code, set up projects, deploy to production, and debug production issues using the filesystem, Git, and Docker.
- **Three Phases of AI Coding**: Articulated a widely-referenced framework — (1) autocomplete (Copilot/Cursor), (2) agentic development (current phase), and (3) fully automated development — describing the evolution of AI in software engineering.

## Vision & Philosophy

Zach's central thesis is that coding is nearly solved and that the ultimate bottleneck for AI will be humans' ability to clearly express intent. He believes the terminal's core form factor — time-based, text-oriented input and output — is uniquely suited for agentic work.

On competition: Warp competes not on cost but on the quality of the holistic product experience. The company has pivoted from a fixed subscription to a consumption-based pricing model as model providers like Anthropic and OpenAI move into the application layer.
