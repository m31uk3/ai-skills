## Background

Scott Breitenother is a data leader and entrepreneur who co-founded Kilo Code alongside Sid Sijbrandij, co-founder and executive chair of GitLab. Before Kilo, Scott built Brooklyn Data Company from his apartment into a 100-person data consultancy before a successful exit. His career spans consulting and analytics leadership, including helping shape modern data practices at Casper by blending analytics, engineering, and organizational design.

## Key Contributions

- **Kilo Code**: Co-founded the fastest-growing open-source coding agent, which has surpassed 750,000 downloads and reached the #1 position on OpenRouter. The platform processes more than 6.1 trillion tokens per month and provides access to more than 500 AI models.
- **Brooklyn Data Company**: Founded and scaled a data consultancy from a solo operation to 100 employees, culminating in a successful acquisition.
- **Locally Optimistic**: Co-founded this community for data professionals, advocating for AI-native workflows that empower small teams to move faster.

## Kilo Code — Key Details

- **Funding**: Raised $8 million in seed funding led by Cota Capital, with participation from Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black.
- **Product**: Open-source AI coding assistant integrating into VS Code and JetBrains IDEs. Features include code generation from natural language, automated debugging, semantic search, memory context, parallel agents, one-click deployment, AI-powered code review, and cloud-based agents.
- **Co-founder**: Sid Sijbrandij (co-founder & executive chair of GitLab), who stepped down from his CEO role at GitLab in December 2024 and partnered with Scott in September 2025.
