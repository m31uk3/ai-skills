## Background

Harrison Chase is the creator of LangChain, the most popular open-source framework for building applications powered by large language models (LLMs). A Harvard graduate in statistics and computer science, Harrison's career before LangChain included leading the Machine Learning team at Robust Intelligence (focused on testing and validation of ML models) and leading the entity linking team at Kensho Technologies, a fintech AI startup later acquired by S&P Global.

## Key Contributions

- **LangChain framework**: Created the de-facto standard open-source Python/TypeScript framework for LLM application development, supporting chains, agents, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and tool use.
- **LangSmith**: Launched in July 2023, this observability platform helps developers monitor, debug, and fine-tune LLM chains and agents in production.
- **LangChain the company**: Formalized LangChain as a venture-backed company in January 2023, announced seed funding in April 2023, and has since built a dedicated team around the framework.

## Leadership & Vision

Harrison describes himself as a product-centric CEO. His approach to innovation emphasizes collaboration, modularity, and developer-first principles — building tools that foster creativity without overwhelming users with complexity. He is widely described as a legend in the agent ecosystem and the product visionary who first connected LLMs with tools and actions.
