What is this tool?

This is an interactive self-reflection aid based on Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT). It helps you identify common patterns of distorted thinking — automatic thought habits that can amplify negative emotions and drive unhelpful behaviors.

The Thought–Emotion–Behavior Cycle

At the core of CBT is a simple but powerful model: your thoughts interpret events, which shape your emotions, which drive your behaviors — and those behaviors feed back into new thoughts. Cognitive distortions live in the thoughts layer. They're the interpretive patterns that collapse nuance into extremes, amplify negative feelings, and narrow your behavioral options.

By naming the distortion, you create space between the automatic thought and your response — space where self-compassion, balanced evidence, and more adaptive choices can emerge.

The Ten Distortions

David D. Burns identified ten common cognitive distortions in his landmark work. Each represents a systematic error in thinking — not a character flaw, but a habit that can be noticed, named, and gently questioned.

The Ten Ways to Untwist

Burns also provided ten counter-techniques — practical methods for challenging distorted thoughts. They include examining evidence, the double-standard method, experimental testing, thinking in shades of gray, and cost-benefit analysis. Each is mapped in the glossary.

How to use this

  • Notice a thought that's causing strong negative emotion.
  • Use the Identify tab to walk through the drill-down.
  • Read the result — name the pattern, see an example, try the reframe.
  • Use the Glossary tab for full definitions and techniques.
